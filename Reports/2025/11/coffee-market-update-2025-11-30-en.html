üî¨ Structural Change: Brazil Robusta Production Surge

According to Bloomberg analysis, Brazil's Robusta production has increased over 81% in the past decade. As traditional Arabica growing regions face severe droughts and high temperatures due to climate change, farmers are transitioning to Robusta varieties that are more heat-tolerant and disease-resistant.

StoneX coffee market analysis manager Fernando Maximiliano noted that "climate issues and Arabica losses, rather than increased demand, have been the main drivers of Robusta growth." This represents a strategic variety shift as Brazil works to maintain its position as the world's largest coffee supplier.

Deep Dive: Structural Impact of Climate Change on the Coffee Market

üìä Key Summary

According to Bloomberg reporting, climate change is causing structural shifts in global coffee market variety composition. While the milder-tasting Arabica has traditionally dominated premium markets, deteriorating growing conditions are accelerating the shift to heat-resistant Robusta.

2. Changing Consumer Trends

According to Euromonitor International's Matthew Barry, Global Insights Manager, younger consumers are less interested in coffee origin or roasting methods. They prefer customized beverages with milk, creamers, and syrups, which mask the original bean flavor.

This creates an environment where Robusta's bitterness becomes less important, and as coffee prices continue to rise, consumers are increasingly likely to shift to more affordable Robusta-based products.

3. Implications for Markets

Opportunity Factors

Robusta Blending Expansion

There is room for increased Robusta content in RTD (Ready-to-Drink) and instant coffee markets. Utilization is expected to increase particularly in value-oriented product lines targeting younger consumers.

Vietnam Direct Import Expansion

Given Vietnam Robusta's improved quality and price competitiveness, expanding direct imports from Vietnam could be an effective cost-reduction strategy for roasters.

Risk Factors

Arabica Scarcity Intensification

If high-quality Arabica scarcity intensifies in specialty markets, premium bean price pressure will increase. Cost burden on specialty roasters is inevitable.

Consumer Price Sensitivity

If continuous coffee price increases trigger consumer price sensitivity, downgrading from specialty to commercial coffee may occur.
