Coffee Futures Market Weekly Update

ICE Inventory Plunge and Tariff Negotiation Expectations Drive Continued Strength

November 9, 2025

Market Overview

Executive Summary

This week, the coffee futures market showed strength as ICE stocks plunged to their lowest level in 1.75 years. While the US maintains its 50% tariff on Brazilian coffee, ICE monitored Arabica stocks fell to 418,203 bags as of Thursday, and Robusta stocks also hit 3.5-month lows at 5,926 lots.

On November 7, December Arabica (KCZ25) closed at 407.35 cents, up +9.70 cents (+2.44%) from the previous day, and on November 8, March (KCH26) rose to 385.85 cents, up +1.79% week-over-week. Expectations for tariff relief formed after talks between Brazil's President Lula and President Trump, but no concrete progress has materialized.

Key Issues and News

1. ICE Inventory and Supply Situation

November 7, 2025

ICE Arabica Stocks Hit 1.75-Year Low

ICE monitored Arabica coffee stocks plunged to 418,203 bags as of Thursday, marking the lowest level in 1.75 years. Robusta stocks also fell to 5,926 lots, a 3.5-month low. The US 50% tariff on Brazilian coffee triggered the sharp decline in ICE stocks, with US importers canceling new Brazilian contracts, intensifying supply tightening. Brazil is a key supplier accounting for about one-third of US green coffee imports.

November 5, 2025

Coffee Prices Rise on Brazil-Vietnam Weather Concerns

March Arabica coffee (KCH26) reached an 8-month high in October and continues its upward trend. Adverse weather in major producing countries Brazil and Vietnam, coffee import tariffs, and growing global demand are combined factors driving prices higher. Market analysts forecast that if March breaks through the $4.00 resistance, it could surge above $4.50.

2. US-Brazil Trade Relations

November 3, 2025

Brazil Considers Ethanol Tariff Cut in Exchange for US Beef-Coffee Tariff Relief

The Brazilian government is negotiating to lower import tariffs on US ethanol in exchange for US relief on the 50% tariff on Brazilian beef and coffee. Currently, US ethanol faces an 18% tariff in Brazil, which the Trump administration has labeled "unfair trade." Brazilian government officials say ethanol tariff concessions are inevitable, but domestic producers worry that US inflows will further depress prices amid existing ethanol oversupply.

November 6, 2025

Tariff Resolution Expectations Rise After Lula-Trump Meeting

On November 4, Brazil's President Lula said his meeting with President Trump was "surprisingly good" and that a "decisive solution" to US-Brazil trade issues could come within days. These remarks raised market expectations for potential relief from the US 50% tariff on Brazilian coffee, also serving as selling pressure from some speculators. However, no specific agreement has been reached yet.

3. Brazil Production and Weather

November 3, 2025

Concerns About Brazil 2025 Harvest and 2026 Crop

Industry experts who visited Brazilian coffee regions firsthand expressed concerns about 2026 crop prospects while reviewing the 2025 harvest season. While 2024 rainfall was relatively adequate, severe drought and high temperatures have returned since mid-2024, intensifying leaf drop. Arabica crops are at critical stress levels and desperately needed September rainfall, but forecasts were uncertain. Experts expect 2026 crop to struggle to exceed 2020 levels, with climate change cited as the biggest obstacle.

November 7, 2025

Brazil Minas Gerais Records 75% of Average Rainfall

Weather service Somar Meteorologia reported that Brazil's largest Arabica growing region Minas Gerais received 33.4mm of rainfall in the week ending October 31, only 75% of the historical average. Continued rainfall deficits are negatively impacting coffee tree flowering and threatening the 2026/27 crop. The market remains concerned that Brazil's dry climate will continue as a long-term supply risk factor.

4. Vietnam Production Outlook

November 7, 2025

Vietnam Jan-Oct Coffee Exports Up 13.4%

Vietnam's National Statistics Office announced that coffee exports from January to October 2025 increased 13.4% year-over-year to 1.31 million tons. Vietnam's 2025/26 season coffee production is forecast to increase 6% year-over-year to 1.76 million tons (29.4 million bags), the highest level in four years. The Vietnam Coffee and Cocoa Association (Vicofa) stated on October 24 that if weather conditions remain favorable, 2025/26 production could increase 10% from the previous crop. Vietnam is the world's largest Robusta coffee producer.

5. Vietnam Typhoon Risk

November 4, 2025

Typhoon Kalmagi Threatens Vietnam Coffee Crops

Coffee prices surged in early November as Typhoon Kalmagi threatened heavy rain and flooding in Vietnam's Central Highlands. On November 3-4, London Robusta November rose 3.51% to $4,683/ton, while January gained 3.37% to $4,693. New York Arabica December also rose 3.72% to 406.65 cents/lb. Vietnam domestic coffee prices spiked to an average of 119,000 dong/kg in major regions on November 4. With production already down 22 million bags due to severe drought in 2024/25, the typhoon threat is intensifying market supply concerns.

6. US Market and Policy

November 7, 2025

US Senate Introduces Bipartisan 'No Coffee Tax Act'

Democratic Senator Catherine Cortez Masto (Nevada) and Republican Senator Rand Paul (Kentucky) have introduced the bipartisan "No Coffee Tax Act." This legislation would eliminate all coffee tariffs imposed during the Trump administration and prohibit future administrations from imposing tariffs on coffee imports. The US relies on imports for over 99% of coffee consumption with only 1% domestic production, so the bill argues tariffs only increase consumer costs without protecting domestic production.

November 7, 2025

US Coffee Chain Market Shows Structural Changes Amid Slowing Growth

According to World Coffee Portal's Project Caf√© USA 2026 report, the US branded coffee shop market reached $58.5 billion with 6.6% sales growth year-over-year, but store count growth slowed to 4.2%. Market leader Starbucks announced closures of hundreds of company-owned stores in September 2025, with growth primarily driven by drive-thru operators (Dutch Bros at 1,055 stores, rapid 7 Brew growth, Black Rock Coffee Bar IPO). Competition is intensifying as China's largest coffee chains Luckin Coffee and Cotti Coffee enter the US market.

7. Brazil Industry News

November 4, 2025

Brazil Cup of Excellence 2025 Achieves Record Scores

40 lots placed in the Brazil Cup of Excellence 2025 auction, with Fazenda Rio Verde's Geisha (experimental processing) achieving the top score of 91.68 points. CarmoCoffees Farm's Luiz Paulo Dias Pereira Filho earned "Legends of Excellence" recognition with 23 Cup of Excellence wins. Brazil's 2025 crop is virtually 100% harvested and is now in storage and commercialization phases.

Supply-Demand Imbalance

Volcafe Analysis: The 2025/26 global Arabica deficit is expected to widen to 8.5 million bags from 5.5 million bags the previous year. This represents a 5th consecutive year of supply shortage, and despite increased Robusta supply, premium Arabica scarcity is expected to intensify.

Brazil 2025 Production Forecast Revision: Brazilian crop forecaster Conab revised its 2025 Arabica forecast down 4.9% from 37 million bags in the May estimate to 35.2 million bags on September 4. Brazil's total coffee production was also revised down 0.9% from 55.7 million bags to 55.2 million bags.

ICO Export Data: The International Coffee Organization (ICO) announced on October 6 that global coffee exports for the current marketing year (October-August) reached 127.92 million bags, up 0.2% year-over-year. While export growth suggests adequate supply on the surface, the concurrent stock decline reflects actually tight supply conditions.

Technical Analysis and Market Sentiment

Key Price Levels

  • Resistance: 400 cents serves as psychological resistance for March. Break above could target 450+ cents
  • Support: 375 cents functions as short-term technical support
  • Moving Averages: Trading above 50-day MA maintains bullish trend. 200-day MA breakout also sustained

Market Sentiment

Bullish sentiment dominates the market. ICE stock plunge, Brazil weather adversity, and continued US tariffs are upward drivers, with speculative long positions expanding. However, potential progress in Brazil-US tariff negotiations remains a short-term volatility factor.

Currency Trends

USD/BRL (Dollar/Brazilian Real)

The USD/BRL exchange rate was 5.33 on November 7, down 0.33% from the previous day. The Brazilian Real has strengthened 0.20% over the past month and 7.02% over the past 12 months. Real strength weakens the dollar-denominated price competitiveness of Brazilian coffee exporters. Traditionally, Real weakness has correlated with coffee price increases, but this negative correlation has weakened recently.

Conclusion

This week, the coffee futures market continued its strength amid plunging ICE stocks and persistent adverse weather in Brazil. December Arabica closed at 407.35 cents on November 7, up about 2.4% for the week, supported by supply tightening concerns as ICE monitored Arabica stocks hit 1.75-year lows at 418,203 bags.

Since the US 50% tariff on Brazilian coffee took effect on August 6, ICE stocks have continuously declined, and supply chain restructuring is underway as US importers cancel new Brazilian contracts. While hopes for tariff relief formed after talks between Brazil's President Lula and President Trump, no concrete progress has been made.

Brazil's 2025 harvest season is complete, but concerns about 2026 crop prospects are growing. Minas Gerais rainfall is only 75% of average, and leaf drop is intensifying due to sustained high temperatures and drought. Industry experts expect the 2026 crop to struggle to exceed 2020 levels.

Meanwhile, Vietnam's 2025/26 production is forecast at 1.76 million tons (29.4 million bags), up 6%, suggesting increased Robusta supply. However, Volcafe's forecast of five consecutive years of Arabica supply shortage (8.5 million bag deficit in 2025/26) is expected to continue supporting prices in the medium to long term.

Going forward, the market will focus on US-Brazil tariff negotiation progress, Brazil Dec-Feb rainfall patterns, La Ni√±a impact severity, and ICE stock trends. In the short term, the key is whether prices break through the 400-cent psychological resistance, with high volatility expected in the 380-420 cent range depending on tariff negotiation news.

With US consumer prices up 12-21% year-over-year, roasters and cafes are facing severe cost pressures, and small operators are exploring survival strategies including in-house roasting, direct trade, and origin diversification. Investors and importers need dollar-cost hedging strategies and options-based volatility management at this juncture.
