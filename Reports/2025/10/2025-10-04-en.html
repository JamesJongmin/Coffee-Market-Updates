Supply-Demand Analysis

December Arabica coffee futures broke through 390 cents this week, the highest level since August, as persistent drought in Brazil's coffee belt and declining ICE stocks intensified supply shortage concerns. The market is now targeting the psychologically significant 400-cent level.

Brazil's Minas Gerais region, which accounts for about 30% of global Arabica production, continues to experience below-average rainfall during the critical September-October flowering period. Agronomists warn that continued drought could significantly impact the 2025/26 crop, with some forecasts already being revised downward.
