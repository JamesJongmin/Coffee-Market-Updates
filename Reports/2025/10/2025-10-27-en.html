Tariff Background Information

September 5, 2025
Coffee Designated "Unobtainable Natural Resource": Trump designated coffee as an "unobtainable natural resource" via executive order, qualifying it for tariff exemption in future trade agreements. However, Brazil has not yet established a trade framework. Source

Breaking News: Trump-Lula Meeting Opens Tariff Relief Negotiation Breakthrough

At the ASEAN summit on October 26 (local time), President Trump and Brazilian President Lula agreed that their negotiation teams would "immediately" begin resolving tariff issues. This is the most concrete negotiation progress since tariffs were imposed in August, and represents a significant variable that could impact the coffee market.

Key Negotiation Details

  • Negotiation teams immediately activated: Treasury Secretary (Scott Bessent), Secretary of State (Marco Rubio), Trade Representative (Jamieson Greer) confirmed attending
  • President Lula's statement: "Confident solution within days", "Exchanged direct phone numbers with Trump"
  • President Trump's position: "Will make a good deal with Brazil", "Hope to complete bilateral negotiations within weeks"
  • Brazil's request: Tariff suspension during negotiations (US response unclear)
  • Coffee industry reaction: Brazilian Coffee Association (ABIC) "Recent meetings positive and optimistic"

Tariff Relief Probability Analysis

Positive Factors:

  • Both leaders agreed to specific negotiation timeline in friendly atmosphere
  • September 5 executive order designated coffee as "unobtainable natural resource" qualifying for exemption
  • US consumer dissatisfaction with rising coffee prices creating negotiation pressure
  • Brazil supplies 30% of US coffee imports (8 million bags annually) as critical supplier
  • Vietnam (20%), Indonesia already have US trade frameworks ‚Üí Brazil exemption negotiation possible
  • Trump celebrated Lula's 80th birthday (10/27), building personal rapport

Negative/Uncertain Factors:

  • Trump linked tariffs to Bolsonaro issue, but Bolsonaro not mentioned at meeting (avoiding political issues)
  • Coffee excluded from July 31 exemption of ~700 items ‚Üí Coffee is "leverage" tool
  • Specific exemption conditions (investment commitments, market opening, etc.) still unclear
  • EU ($600B), Japan ($550B), Korea ($350B) obtained tariff relief with large US investment commitments
  • Brazil maintains close trade ties with China (China approved 183 Brazilian coffee exporters in August)
