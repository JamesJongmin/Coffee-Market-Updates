Supply-Demand Analysis

December Arabica coffee futures maintained elevated price levels at 373.05 cents this week, supported by declining ICE stocks and revised-down Brazil production forecasts. The market remains structurally bullish despite some short-term volatility.

The US 50% tariff on Brazilian coffee, implemented August 6, continues to reshape global coffee trade flows. US importers have largely stopped purchasing Brazilian coffee, redirecting demand to alternative origins including Colombia, Peru, Honduras, and Mexico. However, these origins lack the scale to fully replace Brazil, which accounts for about 30% of US coffee imports.
