Market Outlook and Investment Implications

Short-term Outlook (Next 2 Weeks): Brazil-US trade negotiation progress is the key variable. President Lula's "soon to be resolved" statement and US roasters' stock depletion crisis are creating pressure for negotiation resolution. If tariff elimination is announced, prices could drop sharply to 320-350 cents, but ICE stock tightness (465,910 bags) and Volcafe's deficit forecast are expected to limit declines below 350 cents. If negotiations stall, expect recovery to 400-420 cent levels.

Medium-term Outlook (Next 3-6 Months): Even if tariff issues are resolved, structural supply shortage remains unchanged. Volcafe's 2025/26 Arabica 8.5 million bag deficit forecast (5th consecutive year) and prolonged Brazil drought (flowering damage) will support price floors. Vietnam's production recovery (+6%, 1.76 million tons) will provide some buffer, but has limited impact on the Arabica market. Expect range-bound trading at 330-400 cents.

Long-term Structural Risks: Extreme weather events from climate change are becoming the coffee industry's "new normal." Perfect Daily Grind analyzed that "high price volatility is becoming reality, a situation roasters and traders can no longer avoid." The 2014 research predicting 50% reduction in coffee-suitable land by 2050 is materializing, with warnings that "high-productivity regions" in Brazil and Vietnam may become unsuitable for coffee cultivation in the future. Long-term, expect structurally elevated prices in the 300-500 cent range.
