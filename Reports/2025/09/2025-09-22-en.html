No Coffee Tax Act In-Depth Analysis

Legislative Feasibility and Strategic Implications

September 22, 2025

Executive Summary

The "No Coffee Tax Act," a bipartisan bill introduced by Representatives Don Bacon (R-NE) and Ro Khanna (D-CA), seeks to eliminate tariffs on coffee imports. This analysis examines the bill's legislative prospects amid the political realities of a Republican-majority Congress and potential presidential veto.

Key Findings

  • Low Passage Probability: Republican Congress unlikely to override Trump tariff policy
  • Symbolic Value: Bill serves to educate public and build bipartisan opposition
  • Alternative Pathways: Supreme Court IEEPA ruling or administrative exemptions more likely relief channels
  • Market Impact: Limited near-term price impact; long-term political momentum building

Political Analysis

Congressional Dynamics

With Republicans controlling both chambers of Congress, the bill faces steep odds. While coffee tariffs enjoy bipartisan opposition in principle, breaking with Trump administration trade policy remains politically difficult for Republican members.

Presidential Veto Threat

Even if the bill passed both chambers, a presidential veto is virtually certain. Overriding a veto requires two-thirds majority in both houses - an extremely high bar for trade policy legislation.

Strategic Value

The bill's true value lies in:

  • Building public awareness of tariff costs on consumers
  • Creating political cover for future negotiations
  • Establishing legislative record for potential court challenges
  • Demonstrating bipartisan concern over inflationary trade policies

Alternative Relief Pathways

Supreme Court IEEPA Challenge

The November 5, 2025 Supreme Court hearing on IEEPA tariff authority could invalidate tariffs imposed under emergency powers. Both liberal and conservative justices have expressed skepticism about executive overreach on trade policy.

Administrative Exemptions

The September 5 executive order designating coffee as an "unobtainable natural resource" creates potential exemption pathway for countries with US trade frameworks. Brazil-US bilateral negotiations could yield similar relief.

Market Implications

Near-term: Minimal price impact from legislative activity alone. Market will focus on tariff negotiation and court proceedings.

Long-term: Building political pressure increases probability of eventual tariff relief. Coffee industry lobbying efforts gaining momentum.
