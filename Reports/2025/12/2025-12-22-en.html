Key Observation: March arabica futures plunged 32.20¢ (-8.66%) over four trading sessions from 372.85¢ to 340.65¢, marking the lowest level since mid-August 2024. The selloff accelerated mid-week as Brazilian rainfall forecasts improved and production estimates for 2026/27 were revised upward. Despite the sharp decline, the Dec-Mar spread remains in extreme backwardation at 34.91¢, indicating persistent supply tightness in nearby delivery periods.

Fundamental Analysis

1. Brazilian Production & Weather

Hedgepoint Global Markets released updated 2026/27 Brazilian production forecasts ranging from 71.0-74.4 million bags on December 12, with arabica production estimated at 46.5-49.0 million bags—representing a potential 23-30% year-over-year increase. This optimistic outlook is supported by recent rainfall patterns in key growing regions.

Minas Gerais received 79.8mm of rainfall in the first half of December, 155% of the historical average. This abundant moisture during the critical flowering period (October-December) supports healthy blossom development for the 2026/27 crop. Climatempo forecasts continued "strong and sustained rainfall" through late December, further reinforcing positive crop prospects.

However, Conab's December 4 report projected 2025 production at 56.54 million bags (+2.4% YoY), with robusta reaching a record 20.8 million bags. The 2025/26 harvest outcome remains crucial for near-term supply dynamics.

2. Global Supply-Demand Balance

USDA Foreign Agricultural Service (FAS) released updated global projections on December 19:

  • 2025/26 Production: 178.848 million bags (+2.0% YoY)
  • Consumption: 177.233 million bags (+1.8% YoY)
  • Ending Stocks: 20.148 million bags (-5.4% YoY)
  • Stocks-to-Use Ratio: 11.4% (down from 12.0%)

The projected decline in ending stocks despite production growth suggests continued structural tightness. The market remains vulnerable to supply disruptions, supporting price floors even amid improved crop prospects.

3. Vietnam Exports & Asian Supply

Vietnam Customs reported November 2024 exports of 88,812 tons (+41.0% YoY), valued at $803 million. Cumulative 11-month exports reached 1.374 million tons (+11.8%) worth $7.94 billion (+24.7%). The robust export performance reflects:

  • Strong 2024/25 harvest following favorable weather
  • Competitive pricing versus African origins
  • Elevated global robusta demand due to arabica tightness

However, ICE robusta inventory fell to 4,012 lots (11.5-month low), indicating that Vietnamese supply increases are being absorbed by strong demand rather than accumulating in exchange warehouses.

4. Inventory Dynamics

ICE arabica certified inventory recovered to 439,257 bags as of December 19, up 10.2% from the November 20 low of 398,645 bags. This marks a 7-week high but remains 45% below the 5-year average of approximately 800,000 bags.

The inventory recovery reflects:

  • Seasonal arrival of Brazilian shipments
  • Reduced demand for physical delivery at elevated price levels
  • Improved US-Brazil trade relations reducing tariff uncertainty

Despite the uptick, inventory levels remain critically low by historical standards, supporting the extreme backwardation structure.

5. Regulatory Developments: EUDR Delay

The European Parliament voted 405-242 on December 17 to delay the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) implementation by one year. New deadlines are:

  • Large companies: December 30, 2026
  • Small/micro companies: June 30, 2027

This delay removes near-term compliance pressure on coffee importers and exporters, potentially normalizing trade flows that were disrupted by uncertainty. However, the market must eventually adapt to stricter traceability requirements, which could structurally increase costs for non-compliant origins.

Technical Analysis

1. Price Action & Key Levels

March 2026 arabica (KCH26) closed at 340.65¢, breaking below the critical 350¢ support level that held through most of November and early December. The 8.66% weekly decline represents the sharpest selloff since early September 2024.

Key Technical Levels:

  • Immediate Support: 330-335¢ (August 2024 lows)
  • Immediate Resistance: 360-365¢ (recent consolidation zone)
  • Major Support: 310-315¢ (July 2024 lows)
  • Major Resistance: 390-400¢ (November-December highs)

2. Backwardation Structure

The Dec-Mar spread closed at 34.91¢ in extreme backwardation, slightly narrowed from the 40¢+ levels seen in November but still indicating severe supply tightness for nearby delivery.

Historical context: Normal market conditions typically show a contango of 5-15¢ (deferred contracts trading higher to reflect carrying costs). The current backwardation implies:

  • Strong demand for immediate physical delivery
  • Inventory holders reluctant to sell at current prices
  • Expectations that supply will improve in Q1-Q2 2026

3. Currency Impact: USD/BRL Dynamics

The USD/BRL exchange rate reached 6.07 on December 19, the highest level since early August 2024. Traditionally, Brazilian real weakness (higher USD/BRL) would pressure coffee prices lower as Brazilian producers sell more aggressively to capture favorable exchange rates.

However, the correlation has broken down in recent months. Despite the real's weakness, Brazilian producer margins remain healthy at current coffee price levels, reducing the urgency to sell. This suggests that the market's primary driver has shifted from currency-induced supply pressure to fundamental production outlook and global inventory concerns.

  • Major consuming countries accelerate inventory stockpiling (EUDR preparation)

Base Case Details (50% Probability):

The market has reacted sharply to improved Brazilian rainfall and upward production revisions for 2026/27, but USDA's projected -5.4% decline in ending stocks and persistently tight inventory conditions should limit downside. A 330-360¢ range-bound consolidation for March futures is the most probable scenario.

The critical variable is Brazilian rainfall patterns in January-February 2026. If rainfall continues as forecast, the bullish case (further decline, 30%) becomes more likely. Conversely, if rainfall sharply declines, the bearish case (rebound, 20%) could materialize. CFTC fund positioning changes and ICE inventory trends require close monitoring.
