Executive Summary

On December 4, 2025, the European Parliament adopted an amendment postponing the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) by one year, with an overwhelming vote of 405 to 242. Large operators and traders now face a December 30, 2026 deadline, while SMEs have until June 30, 2027.

Key Message

This postponement is not a reprieve‚Äîit's the final window of opportunity for the Korean coffee industry to prepare. The global coffee supply chain is already bifurcating into 'EUDR-compliant' and 'non-compliant' streams, directly impacting prices and availability in non-EU markets like Korea.

Three Key Challenges for the Korean Market

‚ë† Supply Chain Disruption & Cost Increases: Global traders are investing heavily in EUDR compliance infrastructure, passing these costs through as a 'Green Premium' on green bean prices. Korean importers face price pressure despite not being directly regulated.

‚ë° Export Business Survival: Korean companies exporting mix coffee, RTD beverages to the EU must prove their coffee is deforestation-free by late 2026. Palm oil (used in creamers) is also covered, creating dual compliance challenges.

‚ë¢ ESG & Ethical Consumption Pressure: If Korea becomes a dumping ground for EU-rejected 'deforestation coffee,' companies face significant brand reputation risks as consumer awareness of sustainability issues grows.

EUDR Regulation Deep Dive

üí° Large Operator vs SME: Who Does This Apply To?

EUDR's large/SME distinction applies to operators placing products on the EU market or traders importing into the EU‚Äînot coffee farmers.

Category EU Definition Deadline

Coffee farmers are not directly subject to EUDR. However, they must provide the data (GPS coordinates, traceability information) that EU importers require to continue trading.

Core Compliance Requirements

1. Geolocation Data

Plots under 4 hectares: Single GPS point identifying farm location
Plots 4+ hectares: Polygon mapping of entire farm boundaries required

This data is cross-referenced with satellite imagery to verify no deforestation or degradation occurred on the land after December 31, 2020.

2. Due Diligence Statement (DDS)

Operators placing products on the EU market must submit a DDS before customs clearance. Upon approval, a Reference Number is issued‚Äîwithout it, goods cannot clear customs. Korean exporters must provide complete data to their EU partners.

3. Traceability & Segregation

Physical flow must be tracked from harvest through processing, storage, and export. For blended coffees, if even one bean traces to a deforested area, the entire batch may be rejected or destroyed.

Risk Benchmarking System

The EU Commission will classify countries/regions as 'Low', 'Standard', or 'High' risk.

  • Low-risk countries: Simplified due diligence, lower inspection rates
  • High-risk countries: Enhanced due diligence, higher inspection rates

This classification will influence which origins Korean importers prefer, as high-risk origins face greater customs delay risks.

Market Bifurcation Scenarios

Scenario A: Dumping Ground Effect

EU-rejected volumes from Ethiopia, Uganda, etc. flood into Korea at lower prices. Budget chains embrace these beans for cost savings, but face international criticism and ESG backlash for consuming "deforestation coffee."

Scenario B: Global Synchronization

Major importers and roasters voluntarily prefer EUDR-compliant coffee for brand protection. Global supply shortage drives Korean prices higher in tandem, squeezing smaller roasters' margins.

Scenario C: Dual Strategy (Most Likely)

Large players (Starbucks, Dongsuh Foods, etc.) use 100% traceable coffee and market it as a differentiator. Meanwhile, price-sensitive budget segment continues using non-compliant beans, deepening market polarization.

Strategy for Korean Roasters & Importers

‚ö†Ô∏è Key Understanding: Korea Is NOT Directly Subject to EUDR

Korea is not an EU member state, so there is no legal obligation to comply with EUDR. However, global supply chain restructuring will inevitably affect prices and availability. Our challenge is not regulatory compliance‚Äîit's managing costs and maintaining competitiveness in a changing market.

  • Implement digital traceability systems with EU partner integration

  • Build separate tracking for compound ingredients (palm oil in creamers)

  • Proactively secure EUDR-compliant volumes ‚Üí build premium lineup
  • Share supply chain transparency via QR codes (storytelling)
  • Increase Bird Friendly, Rainforest Alliance certified coffee share
  • Position price increases as 'conscious consumption'

  • Increase Brazil/Colombia (ready) share vs manage Ethiopia (high-risk) exposure
  • Evaluate ESG risks if handling non-compliant volumes
  • Review blend ratio adjustments to absorb cost increases

Conclusion: Prepare for Change, But Avoid Overreaction

EUDR does not impose direct legal obligations on Korean roasters and importers. However, global supply chain restructuring has already begun, and its effects will inevitably reach our market.

The key is 'market change adaptation'‚Äînot 'regulatory compliance'. As EUDR compliance costs are passed through global green bean prices, cost increases are inevitable. Simultaneously, EU-rejected non-compliant volumes may flow into Asian markets.

üìå Key Action Items

1. Immediately: Inquire with key suppliers about EUDR readiness ‚Üí understand pricing scenarios

2. H1 2026: Origin-specific risk assessment, blend adjustment plans, pricing strategy

3. H2 2026: Customer communication preparation, menu/product price adjustments if needed

4. Strategic Decision: Clearly decide whether your brand will pursue "EUDR-compliant premium" or prioritize "price competitiveness"

Overreaction should also be avoided. There's no need to adopt expensive traceability solutions or unnecessarily cut suppliers if you're focused solely on the Korean domestic market. For domestic-only businesses, flexibly adapting to market price changes is the best strategy.

However, if you're targeting the specialty market or prioritizing ESG branding, EUDR can become a differentiation opportunity. "Our coffee doesn't destroy forests" will become an increasingly powerful marketing message.
