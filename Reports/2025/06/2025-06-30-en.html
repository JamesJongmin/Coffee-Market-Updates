Arabica Coffee Futures (KCU25) at Crossroads

Short-term Supply Pressure vs Underlying Support Analysis

June 30, 2025

Introduction: The 300 Cents Battle

The Arabica coffee futures market (ICE KCU25) has reached a psychologically and technically important inflection point, with prices engaged in fierce battle around the 300 cents/pound resistance level. This price action reflects deep tension between two powerful conflicting narratives dominating the market.

On one hand, the market faces immediate pressure from the ongoing Brazil harvest, representing an influx of new supply that drove prices down from over 400 cents/pound at the start of the year.

On the other hand, underlying currents suggest bullish reversal foundations are forming: structurally tight global supply-demand balance, production disruptions in other major origins, and meaningful changes in market participant positioning.

Section 1: The Bearish Case ‚Äì Weight of the Harvest

1.1. Brazil 2025/26 Harvest Progress

Brazil's 2025/26 harvest is underway, with Safras & Mercado reporting 35% completion as of mid-June, matching the 5-year average. Cooxupe reported 24.3% completion as of June 20.

Upgraded Production Outlook: Conab raised its 2025 crop forecast to 55.7 million bags in May, up 7.5% from January and 2.7% above 2024, driven by better-than-expected Robusta yields.

1.2. The Robusta Factor

Brazil's 2025 harvest features record Robusta (Conilon) production. Conab forecasts 18.7 million bags (+27.9% YoY), while Rabobank projects 24.7 million bags. This creates significant substitution pressure with Arabica trading at a 900 BRL/bag premium over Conilon.

1.3. Favorable Harvest Weather

Dry conditions in key growing regions like Minas Gerais are ideal for harvest and quality preservation. Long-range forecasts show diminishing frost risk for coffee-growing areas.

Section 2: The Bullish Case ‚Äì Cracks in the Foundation

2.1. Stressed Brazil Arabica Crop

  • Off-year cycle: 2025/26 is an 'off-year' in biennial production
  • Weather damage: 2024 drought and heat waves impacted flowering
  • Quality concerns: Irregular flowering causing cherry drop

2.2. Other Origins Supply Disruptions

Colombia production decline: USDA forecasts 5.3% decrease to 12.5 million bags for 2025/26 due to heavy rainfall damage.

2.3. Persistent Global Shortage Narrative

Volcafe continues to forecast 8.5 million bag Arabica deficit for 2025/26 - the fifth consecutive year of shortage. USDA data confirms ending stocks at 25-year lows.

Section 3: Market Structure and Positioning
