Weekly Market Summary

Market Summary

The coffee market faced heavy selling pressure this week as supply improvement expectations crystallized. March arabica (KCH26) settled at 296.55 cents on Friday, plunging approximately 10.2% week-over-week to hit a 6-month low not seen since August 2025. March robusta (RMH26) also fell to a 5.75-month low.

Key Catalyst: On Thursday, Conab (Brazil's crop forecasting agency) projected 2026 Brazilian coffee production at a record 66.2 million bags (+17.2% y/y), sharply souring market sentiment. Arabica output is forecast at 44.1M bags (+23.2%) and robusta at 22.1M bags (+6.3%), representing broad-based production gains across all varieties. This surpasses Brazil's previous official record of 63.1M bags set in 2020.

The sell-off began on Tuesday and accelerated through the latter half of the week. Both commercial and non-commercial (speculative) sellers were active simultaneously. On Friday alone, KCH26 posted an intraday range of over 21 cents (315.00 to 293.65). The cash-to-front spread (KCY00 333.84 vs KCH26 296.55) remains at 37 cents, signaling persistent near-term physical supply tightness.

Notably, the forward curve remains in backwardation, indicating that longer-term supply-demand dynamics still lean bullish. On Thursday, Brazil's Trade Ministry reported January coffee exports collapsed -42.4% y/y, triggering short-covering that briefly lifted robusta into positive territory.

Brazil Weather & Production

Origin Focus

Rainfall patterns in Minas Gerais, Brazil's largest arabica-growing region, have normalized dramatically. According to Somar Meteorologia, the week ending January 30 saw 69.8mm of rainfall (117% of the historical average), a stark reversal from the severe drought conditions of early January (29% of average). This precipitation is highly favorable for the bean filling phase of the 2026/27 crop.
