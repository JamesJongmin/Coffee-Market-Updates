Market Overview

Coffee futures extended their slide for a third consecutive week. May arabica (KCK26) struck a 15-month low on Thursday February 19, while ICE robusta hit a 6.25-month low. üáªüá≥ Vietnam ‚Äî Robusta Export Surge Continues

Vietnam's January coffee exports surged to 198,000 MT (+38.3% y/y), maintaining sustained downward pressure on robusta prices. Full-year 2025 exports reached 1.58M MT (+17.5% y/y), and the 2025/26 crop is projected at 1.76M MT (+6% y/y), a 4-year high.

üá®üá¥ Colombia ‚Äî Supply Disruption Offers Partial Support

Colombia's National Federation of Coffee Growers (FNC) reported January production of 893,000 bags (‚àí34% y/y). As the world's second-largest arabica producer, Colombia's sustained shortfall is limiting the downside in arabica prices and widening origin differentials.

üì¶ ICE Inventories & Physical Market Dynamics

ICE-certified arabica inventories recovered from a 1.75-year low of 396,513 bags (Nov 18) to a 3.75-month high of 461,829 bags (Jan 7), before settling around ~426,000 bags currently, with 89.6% held in European warehouses. Robusta inventories also rebounded from a 14-month low.

üí° Physical vs. Futures Divergence

A notable development this week: physical-market premiums (differentials) are widening relative to futures. According to Reuters, this suggests the futures sell-off may have overshot the actual physical supply situation. In other words ‚Äî futures look oversold while nearby arabica physical supply remains relatively tight.

üá∫üá∏ USDA $1B Specialty Crop Assistance (Feb 19)

The USDA announced a $1 billion Specialty Crop Assistance Program covering U.S. coffee farmers in Hawaii, Puerto Rico, and California, designed to offset market disruptions, elevated input costs, and import competition. Near-term price impact is limited, but the policy signals a strategic push to expand domestic U.S. coffee production infrastructure.

üåê Global Supply Outlook

USDA FAS projects 2025/26 world coffee production at 178.85 million bags (+2.0% y/y), with arabica down 4.7% to 95.5M bags and robusta up 10.9% to 83.3M bags. Ending stocks are forecast at 20.15M bags (‚àí5.4% y/y), remaining historically low.
