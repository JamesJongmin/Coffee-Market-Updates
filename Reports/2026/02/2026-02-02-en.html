Weekly Market Summary

Key Developments

Coffee markets experienced a sharp correction this week under dual pressure from Brazil rain forecasts and a broad commodity selloff. March arabica (KCH26) closed Friday at 332.25 cents, marking a 5.5-month low. The contract fell 23.05 cents (-6.49%) on a weekly basis.

Key Driver: Friday's precious metals flash crash amplified selling pressure across commodities. President Trump's nomination of Kevin Warsh as next Fed Chair triggered a historic plunge in gold (-9% to -13%) and silver (-31%, worst single-day drop since 1980). The resulting dollar strength weighed heavily on dollar-denominated commodities including coffee.

From a coffee-specific perspective, forecasts for steady rains in Brazil's Minas Gerais region over the next week acted as the primary bearish catalyst. As Brazil's largest arabica-producing area, improved moisture conditions could benefit the 2026/27 crop development, prompting profit-taking from recent highs.

Supply fundamentals remain structurally tight despite the price correction. ICE certified arabica stocks stand at 462,000 bags, a 2.5-month high but still 54% below year-ago levels (990,000 bags). However, the market is increasingly pricing in expectations for a strong 2026/27 Brazilian crop, weakening near-term price support.

Fundamental Analysis

Supply & Demand

Brazil Production Outlook

Conab (Brazil's crop forecasting agency) raised its 2025 total coffee production estimate to 56.54 million bags on December 4, up 2.4% from the September estimate of 55.20 million bags. Sucafina's recent Brazil crop tour projects 2026/27 production at 49.7 million bags of arabica and 25.9 million bags of robusta (total 75.6 million bags), with record output expected from Cerrado and Rondonia regions.

Cecafé reported that Brazil's December green coffee exports fell 18.4% y/y to 2.86 million bags, with arabica down 10% to 2.6 million bags and robusta plunging 61% to 222,147 bags. This reflects the lingering impact of U.S. tariffs that peaked at 50% from August through November.

Vietnam Robusta Supply

Vietnam's National Statistics Office reported 2025 coffee exports jumped 17.5% y/y to 1.58 million metric tons, with export revenues reaching a record $8.4 billion (+60.7% y/y). Production for 2025/26 is forecast to rise 6% to 29.4 million bags, a 4-year high. VICOFA indicated output could increase by 10% if weather conditions remain favorable. This abundant robusta supply continues to cap upside potential for robusta prices.

Inventory Recovery: ICE-monitored arabica inventories recovered from a 1.75-year low of 398,645 bags (Nov 20) to a 2.5-month high of 461,829 bags (Jan 14). Robusta stocks also rebounded from a 1-year low of 4,012 lots (Dec 10) to a 1.75-month high of 4,609 lots (Jan 24).

Macro & Commodity Trends

Cross-Asset Impact

Friday's commodity markets experienced a historic precious metals-led selloff. President Trump's nomination of Kevin Warsh as next Fed Chair was the primary catalyst. Warsh is viewed as a hawkish policymaker likely to prioritize inflation control, shifting expectations toward "higher for longer" interest rates.

Technical Analysis

Chart Patterns

March arabica breached the 200-day moving average (344.42 cents), signaling a technically bearish posture. Friday's close at 332.25 cents represents a 5.5-month low, with the key 330 cents support level now being tested.

Key Technical Levels:
• Resistance: 344¢ (200-day MA), 350¢ (psychological), 365¢ (nearby high)
• Support: 330¢ (nearby low), 320¢ (downside target), 310¢ (September contract level)

CFTC positioning data shows managed money (hedge funds) maintains sizeable long positions, suggesting further downside could trigger additional liquidation. The nearby spread remains in backwardation but has narrowed, indicating easing physical market tightness.
