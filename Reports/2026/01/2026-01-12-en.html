Brazil Rain Forecast Eases Drought Concerns

Jan 9

According to Somar Meteorologia, rainfall is expected across central Brazil over the next week, providing some relief from severe drought conditions. The market reacted strongly to the short-term forecast, triggering speculative position liquidation, though long-term moisture deficits remain a concern for the 2026/27 crop.

Vietnam 2025 Exports Jump 17.5%

Jan 6

Vietnam's National Statistics Office reported 2025 coffee exports surged 17.5% y/y to 1.58 million metric tons (MMT). As the world's largest robusta producer, this signals increased supply pressuring the robusta market. The 2025/26 crop is forecast at 1.76MMT (+6% y/y), a 4-year high, with favorable weather conditions potentially supporting even higher output according to Vicofa.

ICE Inventories at 462K Bags, Historic Lows Persist

Jan 8

ICE-monitored arabica inventories rose to 461,829 bags on January 8, up slightly from the prior week but still representing just a 2.5-month high. After hitting a 1.75-year low of 398,645 bags on November 20, inventories have recovered modestly but remain near historic lows. Robusta inventories also recovered from a 1-year low of 4,012 lots (12/10) to 4,278 lots (12/23-24), but tight supply conditions persist.

US Brazilian Coffee Purchases Plunge 52%

Jan 6

US purchases of Brazilian coffee plummeted 52% y/y to 983,970 bags during August-October due to Trump administration tariffs. US importers shifted to alternative origins including Peru, Honduras, Mexico, and Ethiopia. The structural shift in supply chains is intensifying US inventory tightness and could support medium-term price pressures despite short-term correction.

Minas Gerais Rainfall at 67% of Average

Jan 6

Somar Meteorologia reported that Minas Gerais, Brazil's largest arabica-producing state, received 47.9mm of rainfall during the week ended January 2, just 67% of the historical average. While additional rain is forecast over the next week, cumulative moisture deficits remain a concern for the 2026/27 crop. The market responded positively to the near-term forecast, but whether flowering-stage moisture will be sufficient requires monitoring over the next 2-3 weeks.

Conab Raises 2025 Production to 56.54M Bags

Dec 4

Conab, Brazil's crop forecasting agency, raised its 2025 total coffee production estimate 2.4% from the September forecast of 55.20M bags to 56.54M bags. This signals adequate near-term supply and contributed to price correction. However, concerns about the 2026/27 crop due to drought remain a medium-term price support factor.

2025/26 Production Forecast Up 6%

Jan 6

Vietnam's 2025/26 coffee production is projected to rise 6% y/y to 1.76MMT (29.4M bags), a 4-year high. The Vietnam Coffee and Cocoa Association (Vicofa) stated on October 24 that if favorable weather conditions persist, production could increase 10% from the previous crop year. This represents supply pressure on the robusta market and limits arabica-robusta price differential expansion.

Export Prices Rise, Farmer Sales Limited

Jan 9

According to traders in the Central Highlands, farmers are selling at 114,000-115,000 dong/kg ($4.33-4.36), but despite high prices, sales volumes remain limited. Some farmers are delaying sales anticipating further price increases, with the main harvest expected to begin in February.

2024/25 Season Hits 14.87M Bags, 33-Year High

Oct 8

The Colombian Coffee Growers Federation (FNC) announced that the 2024/25 season (ended September) produced 14.87M bags, up 17% y/y and the highest output in 33 years. According to USDA forecasts, 2025/26 global coffee production is expected to reach a record high, but with structural changes: arabica production declining 4.7% while robusta surges 10.9%. Brazil's arabica decline combined with Vietnam's robusta increase suggests continued tightness in the premium coffee market.

Notably, ending stocks are forecast to decline 5.4% despite production growth. This indicates demand growth exceeding supply growth, which should provide medium-term price support.

ICO Export Data

The International Coffee Organization (ICO) reported on November 7 that global coffee exports for the current marketing year (Oct-Sep) totaled 138.658M bags, down 0.3% y/y. Despite production growth forecasts, the export decline suggests increased domestic consumption in major producing countries or inventory accumulation tendencies.
