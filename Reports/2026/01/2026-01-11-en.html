üí∞ FX Hedging Necessity

The 2025 experience highlights the importance of FX risk management. The exchange rate surge during peak import periods (Feb-Jun) significantly increased the burden on roasters.

Implications of H2 Import Decline

The sharp decline in Brazil Q3-Q4 import volume (-18.3%, -9.2%) can be interpreted in two ways:

Interpretation 1: Sufficient Inventory
  • H1 bulk purchases created adequate inventory
  • Lower need for additional purchases
  • Priority on depleting existing stock
Interpretation 2: High Price Avoidance
  • USD $7.5-7.9/kg still considered expensive
  • Waiting for further price drops
  • Securing only minimum required volumes

In reality, both factors likely combined. While H1 purchases secured some inventory, roasters maintained a cautious approach as additional purchases were still considered expensive.

Q1 2026 Inventory Outlook

‚ö†Ô∏è Potential Q1 2026 Inventory Pressure

Due to H2 2025 import decline (especially from Brazil), inventory shortage risks may emerge in Q1 2026.

If coffee prices remain elevated in Q1 2026 and inventory runs low, roasters may be forced to make high-cost purchases at unwanted timing.

Conversely, if prices fall in early 2026, losses from price gaps with 2025 high-cost inventory will become more apparent.

üí° Roastery Strategy

Brazil Coffee Procurement Strategy

üìå Strategic Recommendations

1. Dollar-Cost Averaging

  • Avoid lump-sum bulk purchases
  • Optimize average cost through monthly/quarterly distribution
  • Maintain flexibility to pause during price spikes
  • 2025 Lesson: Feb-Jun concentrated buying resulted in high-cost inventory burden

2. FX Risk Hedging

  • Forward contracts 3-6 months ahead for planned bulk purchases
  • Review currency options if KRW/USD exceeds 1,500
  • Negotiate USD-fixed price contracts
  • 2025 Lesson: Average 7%p additional burden from FX

3. Strategic Combination with Colombia

  • Brazil: Bulk procurement on dips, for base blends
  • Colombia: Stable base volume, for premium blends
  • Increase Colombia share when Brazil is expensive (2025 Q3 pattern)
  • Prioritize Colombia when price gap < $1/kg

4. Inventory Management Principles

  • Optimal inventory: 3-4 months coverage
  • Aggressive buying on dips, minimal on spikes
  • Strict FIFO (First-In-First-Out) discipline
  • Allocate high-cost purchases to premium product lines first

5. Pricing Policy

  • Gradual cost pass-through (avoid sudden spikes)
  • Enhanced customer communication (explain global market dynamics)
  • Dual-tier structure: premium vs. budget lines
  • Long-term fixed-price options for existing customers
