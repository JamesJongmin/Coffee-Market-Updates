Executive Summary

Coffee futures prices are not simply a function of supply and demand. This analysis quantifies the relative importance of three key variables in explaining coffee price movements, based on 30 months of data from H2 2023 to 2025.

üí° Key Finding

The USD/BRL exchange rate explains 75.4% of coffee price variation. As Brazil is the world's largest coffee producer (~40%), a weaker Real reduces dollar-denominated export revenues ‚Üí lower export incentives ‚Üí reduced supply ‚Üí price increase.

Interestingly, CFTC speculative positions and NDVI crop index alternate in influence across time periods. In the first half of the year, crop conditions (NDVI) become important during Brazil's rainy season, while speculative trading (CFTC) dominates in the second half after harvest confirmation.

üåø Seasonality Interpretation

H1 covers the latter part of Brazil's rainy season and early dry season, when coffee trees undergo flowering and fruit development. During this time, NDVI (crop index) is more sensitively reflected in prices.

H2 covers late dry season and harvest period (May-Sep). As harvest volumes are confirmed, crop uncertainty decreases, and speculative trading and exchange rate movements drive prices.

Practical Implications

Trading Strategy Recommendations

üí± 1. Strengthen FX Monitoring

Since USD/BRL explains 75% of coffee prices, monitoring exchange rate drivers‚ÄîBCB policy, current account, political risk‚Äîis essential.

Action: Consider long positions when FX exceeds 5.5; prepare for coffee price increases during rapid Real depreciation.

üìä 2. Utilize CFTC Reports

In H2, monitor Non-Commercial position changes in the CFTC Commitment of Traders report. Extreme positions (all-time high long/short) may signal reversals.

Action: Check weekly COT reports every Friday; watch for overheating when net long exceeds 60,000 contracts.

üåø 3. Focus on Crop Conditions in H1

From January to June, focus on Brazilian weather and NDVI data. Key sources include NASA MODIS, Copernicus satellite data, and Conab crop reports.

Action: Monitor Jan-Mar rainy season rainfall, Apr-Jun dry season onset, and supply concerns when NDVI falls below 0.6.
