Weekly Market Summary

Key Developments

Coffee markets rallied this week as dollar weakness triggered broad-based short covering across commodities. March arabica (KCH26) closed Friday at 350.90 cents, up +3.20 cents (+0.92%), while March robusta surged +116 (+2.88%) to post a 1.5-month high.

Key Catalyst: The dollar index (DXY) slumped to 98.45 on Friday, marking a 3.5-month low. The dollar weakness prompted short covering across most commodities, including coffee. The Brazilian real also rallied to a 2.25-month high against the dollar, discouraging export sales by Brazilian producers.

Shrinking Brazilian coffee exports are supportive of prices. According to Cecaf√©, Brazil's total December green coffee exports fell -18.4% y/y to 2.86 million bags, with arabica exports down -10% y/y to 2.6 million bags and robusta exports plunging -61% y/y to 222,147 bags.

However, the recovery in ICE coffee inventories is weighing on prices. ICE-monitored arabica inventories fell to a 1.75-year low of 398,645 bags on November 20, but recovered to a 2.5-month high of 461,829 bags last Wednesday. ICE robusta inventories also rose from a 1-year low of 4,012 lots on December 10 to 4,609 lots on Friday.

Brazil Weather Update

Crop Development Conditions

Below-average rainfall in Brazil, the world's largest arabica producer, continues to support prices. Somar Meteorologia reported that Brazil's largest arabica coffee-growing area, Minas Gerais, received 33.9 mm of rain during the week ended January 16, or 53% of the historical average.

Global Supply Dynamics

Production & Export Trends

Vietnam posted record coffee export earnings in the 2024/25 crop year. According to VICOFA, Vietnam's coffee export revenue reached $8.4 billion, up 55.5% y/y. Export volume exceeded 1.5 million tonnes (+1.8% y/y), with the average export price surging 52.7% y/y to $5,610 per tonne. More recently, VICOFA announced that 2025 total exports reached a record $8.92 billion, up over 58% y/y.

Vietnam 2025/26 Outlook: VICOFA projects production to increase 5-10% y/y thanks to favorable weather and high domestic prices encouraging farmer investment. USDA FAS forecasts Vietnam's 2025/26 output at 1.76 million tonnes (29.4 million bags), a 4-year high.

Optimism prevails for Brazil's 2026/27 crop. StoneX forecasts Brazil's coffee production at 70.7 million bags, up 13.5% y/y to a record high. Arabica is expected at 47.2 million bags (+29.3%), while robusta is seen at 23.5 million bags (-8.9%). Rabobank projects a global surplus of 7-10 million bags in 2026/27, which could push arabica prices down by approximately one-third by late 2026.
