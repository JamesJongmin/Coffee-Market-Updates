UCO (Used Cooking Oil) Market Trends

Analyzing the supply-demand structure and pricing trends of the UCO market, a key feedstock for global biodiesel and SAF production.

Global UCO Supply & Demand Global
Global UCO collection volume is estimated at approximately 7-9 million MT annually, with China, Southeast Asia, and Europe as primary sources.
Region Collection (M MT/yr) Key Features
China 3.5-5.0 Largest producer & exporter, quality standardization in progress
Southeast Asia 1.2-1.8 Palm oil-based UCO growing in Indonesia & Malaysia
Europe 1.0-1.5 Strict collection systems, RED III regulatory impact
North America 0.6-0.8 Policy-driven demand via RFS & LCFS
Asia UCO Market Asia
The Asian UCO market is rapidly evolving with China's export policies and expanding collection infrastructure in Southeast Asia.
  • China: Government "food waste resourceization" policy boosting collection rates, increasing export volume volatility
  • Indonesia: Building palm oil-based UCO collection systems, expanding B40 biodiesel blending mandate
  • Japan/Korea: Premium UCO pricing established, surging SAF feedstock demand in Japan
  • India: Growing food service industry driving UCO collection potential
  • Price drivers: EU RED III origin requirements tightening, increasing Asian UCO price volatility
UCO Market Key Insight
The implementation of EU RED III (Renewable Energy Directive III) has significantly strengthened UCO origin traceability and certification requirements, restricting Chinese UCO exports to Europe. This creates a polarization effect: oversupply in Asian markets and price increases in European markets. Meanwhile, in the US market, RFS (Renewable Fuel Standard) and California's LCFS (Low Carbon Fuel Standard) policies continue to drive UCO-based biodiesel demand, triggering a fundamental reshaping of global UCO trade flows.

Tallow (Animal Fat) Market Trends

Analyzing global supply-demand dynamics and pricing trends of Tallow, a primary feedstock for biodiesel and SAF.

Global Tallow Supply Structure Global
Tallow is a livestock industry by-product with global annual production of approximately 9-10 million MT.
Region Production (M MT/yr) Notes
United States 2.5-3.0 World's largest Tallow producer, surging RD feedstock demand
Brazil 1.2-1.5 #1 in cattle slaughter globally, expanding exports

Asia Tallow Import Trends Asia
In Asian markets, Tallow demand is surging for biofuel feedstock alongside traditional food and industrial uses.
  • Singapore: Asia's Tallow trading hub, Neste and multiple refiners operating bio-refineries
  • Korea: Expanding HVO (Hydrotreated Vegetable Oil) facility investments, growing Tallow imports
  • Japan: Intensifying competition for Tallow feedstock supply driven by SAF production targets
  • China: New biofuel demand emerging alongside traditional soap and chemical feedstock uses
  • Price premium: Biofuel-grade Tallow commands $50-100/MT premium over standard grades
Tallow Market Key Insight
The rapid expansion of Renewable Diesel (RD) production capacity in the US has caused Tallow demand to surge. As of 2025, US RD production capacity reaches approximately 6 billion gallons/year, with a significant portion using Tallow as feedstock. This keeps domestic US Tallow prices at historically elevated levels, driving legacy soap and chemical industries to accelerate substitution with alternative feedstocks (palm oil, UCO). In Asia, Australian and New Zealand Tallow exports to the region are increasing, but supply tightness is expected to persist due to price competition with the US market.

SAF (Sustainable Aviation Fuel) Market Trends

Analyzing the current state and outlook of the SAF market, the cornerstone of aviation industry decarbonization.

As of 2025, global SAF production represents only ~0.5-1% of total jet fuel demand, but rapid growth is expected.
Item Figure Notes

Price vs conventional jet fuel 2-4x premium Premium narrowing trend

Asia SAF Market Trends Asia
Analyzing SAF mandatory blending policies and production facility investment across Asian countries.
  • Japan: Legislated 10% SAF blending mandate by 2030. ENEOS, COSMO and other refiners accelerating SAF facility investment
  • Korea: SAF blending mandate from 2027, SK Energy & GS Caltex preparing production
  • Singapore: Neste's largest Asian SAF production facility operational, ~1M MT annual capacity
  • China: SAF demand growing with domestic aviation carbon emission reduction targets, Sinopec and other SOEs participating
  • India: Early-stage SAF adoption amid rapid aviation industry growth, government incentive policies in preparation
SAF Market Key Insight
With EU's ReFuelEU Aviation regulation (2% SAF by 2025, 6% by 2030, 70% by 2050 mandatory blending) taking effect, global SAF demand is structurally increasing. Currently, most SAF production relies on the HEFA (Hydroprocessed Esters and Fatty Acids) process, which uses UCO and Tallow as primary feedstocks. Therefore, SAF market growth directly impacts UCO and Tallow supply-demand dynamics. In the longer term, the commercialization pace of next-generation technologies such as e-fuels (Power-to-Liquid) and Alcohol-to-Jet will be a critical variable. In Asia, Japan is emerging as the SAF policy leader, followed by Korea and Singapore.
UCO/Tallow & Corn/Soybean Oil Correlation
UCO and Tallow are closely correlated with corn and soybean oil markets. We analyze the substitution dynamics and price linkage mechanisms within the biofuel feedstock market.
Soybean Oil ‚Üí UCO Price Linkage
Soybean oil is a primary biodiesel feedstock. When soybean oil prices rise, UCO emerges as a substitute feedstock, driving UCO demand and prices higher. The two markets show a high correlation coefficient of 0.7-0.85.
Corn ‚Üí Ethanol ‚Üí Biofuel
Corn is the key feedstock for US ethanol production. Corn price fluctuations affect ethanol profitability, which indirectly impacts Tallow and UCO demand through profitability comparisons with biodiesel and RD.
RIN/LCFS Credit Connection
US RFS RIN (Renewable Identification Numbers) prices and California LCFS credit prices are the critical variables determining overall biofuel feedstock (UCO, Tallow, soybean oil, corn) price levels.
Food vs Fuel Competition
The growing use of soybean oil and corn for biofuel feedstock raises food price concerns. This "food vs fuel" issue elevates the strategic value of waste oils like UCO and Tallow.

US Policy Deep-Dive

US Biofuel Policy Deep-Dive: 45Z Tax Credit & Feedstock Shift

US biofuel producers are increasingly turning to domestic feedstocks as restrictive biofuel and trade policies limit the use of foreign materials. A seismic shift with major implications for UCO/Tallow manufacturers and traders.

45Z Clean Fuel Production Tax Credit ‚Äî Key Changes Policy
The 45Z tax credit launched in 2025 to replace the $1/USG blender's tax credit (BTC), triggering a fundamental restructuring of the biofuel feedstock market. In late January 2026, the White House OMB concluded its review of the 45Z proposed rule (Biodiesel Magazine, 1/26). In early February, Treasury released new 45Z guidance bringing some clarity, though producers continue seeking additional detail on implementation (RSM US, 2/6).
Timeline Policy Change UCO/Tallow Impact
2025 Launch 45Z tax credit replaces $1/USG BTC Foreign UCO ineligible for credits ‚Üí Chinese UCO imports plunge
2025 Q3 Import tariffs disrupt foreign Tallow trade 50% tariff on Brazilian Tallow, 10% on Australian Tallow
2026 onwards 45Z applies exclusively to North American feedstocks All foreign feedstocks excluded from credits ‚Üí domestic shift accelerates
2026 (proposed) EPA proposes halving RIN credits for foreign feedstock fuels Significant margin risk for imported feedstock users
Feedstock Import Trends Trade
Feedstock imports slowed dramatically in H2 2025 with new 45Z rules and import tariffs.
  • Chinese UCO: January-October 2025 shipments fell 65% YoY. 45Z ineligibility is the direct cause
  • Brazilian Tallow: After 50% tariffs in August, Q4 volumes plunged below 22M lbs (from record 154M lbs in June)
  • Australian Tallow: Maintained strong flows until October despite 10% tariffs, but expected to decline as 45Z restrictions take effect
  • Historical context: Heavy imports since early 2023 sidelined domestic supply, with Chinese UCO flooding the market and Brazilian/Australian Tallow following suit
  • Peak consumption: Record-high UCO consumption in July 2024; Tallow hit its own record in July 2025
Soybean Oil-Led Domestic Feedstock Shift Feedstock
The Trump administration's push to curb imports aims to strengthen domestic feedstocks, particularly soybean oil, in US biofuel production.
  • Crush capacity: Grown 13% since 2022 to 2.97B bu/yr with 10 new crush plants
  • Monthly records: Record 227.6M bu crush in October 2025, with multiple monthly records throughout the year
  • Biodiesel feedstock share: Soybean oil accounts for ~70% of biodiesel feedstock mix in 2024
  • RD/SAF divergence: Renewable diesel & SAF industries target lower-carbon feedstocks, leaving soybean oil demand below crusher expectations
  • 2026 outlook: 45Z credit premium for soybean oil expected to rise as regulators stop considering land-use impacts, narrowing gap with waste feedstocks
  • vs Canola: Canola oil receives ~50% lower credit value ‚Üí soybean oil gains competitive advantage

Key Implications for UCO/Tallow Manufacturers
Foreign UCO is effectively being phased out of the US market. Starting 2026, 45Z will apply exclusively to North American feedstocks, structurally reducing Chinese and Southeast Asian UCO flows to the US. For Asian UCO manufacturers, this means a strategic pivot toward European and intra-Asian markets is essential. For Australian and Brazilian Tallow exporters, diversifying to new destinations (Asia, Middle East) becomes critical. Conversely, US domestic Tallow and soybean oil suppliers stand to benefit from sustained premium pricing. The EPA's proposed record-high 2026-2027 blending mandates and half-RIN provision, if finalized, would further strengthen domestic feedstock demand. However, delays in finalizing biofuel mandates and lack of 45Z guidance remain key uncertainty risks ‚Äî close monitoring of Q1 2026 policy developments is essential.

Price trends: US soybean oil futures closed at 49.44¬¢/lb on December 30, down 14% from the July peak of 57.54¬¢/lb, making soybean oil more attractive compared to waste-based feedstocks. Agriculture groups claim expanded crush capacity and record production will provide sufficient soybean oil to meet biofuel demand, reducing reliance on imported feedstocks.

US Biofuel Policy Deep-Dive: 45Z Tax Credit & Feedstock Shift

US biofuel producers are increasingly turning to domestic feedstocks as restrictive biofuel and trade policies limit the use of foreign materials. A seismic shift with major implications for UCO/Tallow manufacturers and traders.

45Z Clean Fuel Production Tax Credit ‚Äî Key Changes Policy
The 45Z tax credit launched in 2025 to replace the $1/USG blender's tax credit (BTC), triggering a fundamental restructuring of the biofuel feedstock market.
Timeline Policy Change UCO/Tallow Impact

Feedstock Import Trends Trade
Feedstock imports slowed dramatically in H2 2025 with new 45Z rules and import tariffs.
  • Chinese UCO: January-October 2025 shipments fell 65% YoY. 45Z ineligibility is the direct cause
  • Brazilian Tallow: After 50% tariffs in August, Q4 volumes plunged below 22M lbs (from record 154M lbs in June)
  • Australian Tallow: Maintained strong flows until October despite 10% tariffs, but expected to decline as 45Z restrictions take effect
  • Historical context: Heavy imports since early 2023 sidelined domestic supply, with Chinese UCO flooding the market and Brazilian/Australian Tallow following suit
  • Peak consumption: Record-high UCO consumption in July 2024; Tallow hit its own record in July 2025
Soybean Oil-Led Domestic Feedstock Shift Feedstock
The Trump administration's push to curb imports aims to strengthen domestic feedstocks, particularly soybean oil, in US biofuel production.
  • Crush capacity: Grown 13% since 2022 to 2.97B bu/yr with 10 new crush plants
  • Monthly records: Record 227.6M bu crush in October 2025, with multiple monthly records throughout the year
  • Biodiesel feedstock share: Soybean oil accounts for ~70% of biodiesel feedstock mix in 2024
  • RD/SAF divergence: Renewable diesel & SAF industries target lower-carbon feedstocks, leaving soybean oil demand below crusher expectations
  • 2026 outlook: 45Z credit premium for soybean oil expected to rise as regulators stop considering land-use impacts, narrowing gap with waste feedstocks
  • vs Canola: Canola oil receives ~50% lower credit value ‚Üí soybean oil gains competitive advantage

Key Implications for UCO/Tallow Manufacturers
Foreign UCO is effectively being phased out of the US market. Starting 2026, 45Z will apply exclusively to North American feedstocks, structurally reducing Chinese and Southeast Asian UCO flows to the US. For Asian UCO manufacturers, this means a strategic pivot toward European and intra-Asian markets is essential. For Australian and Brazilian Tallow exporters, diversifying to new destinations (Asia, Middle East) becomes critical. Conversely, US domestic Tallow and soybean oil suppliers stand to benefit from sustained premium pricing. The EPA's proposed record-high 2026-2027 blending mandates and half-RIN provision, if finalized, would further strengthen domestic feedstock demand. However, delays in finalizing biofuel mandates and lack of 45Z guidance remain key uncertainty risks ‚Äî close monitoring of Q1 2026 policy developments is essential.

Price trends: US soybean oil futures closed at 49.44¬¢/lb on December 30, down 14% from the July peak of 57.54¬¢/lb, making soybean oil more attractive compared to waste-based feedstocks. Agriculture groups claim expanded crush capacity and record production will provide sufficient soybean oil to meet biofuel demand, reducing reliance on imported feedstocks.
